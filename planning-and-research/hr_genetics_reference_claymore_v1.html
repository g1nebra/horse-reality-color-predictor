General notes
HR custom gene Several genes in Horse Reality are custom implementations with no confirmed real-world equivalent. Their allele notation, dominance rules, and interactions are defined entirely by the HR team and can only be referenced from the HR wiki or community sources, not from real equine genetics literature. Known HR-custom or non-standard genes include: Hidden Sabino (Y): recessive, untestable, covers sabino-like patterns of unknown genetic origin. Rabicano (rb): modelled as a simple recessive hidden gene, whereas real-world rabicano genetics are not fully understood. White Markings (WM): a hidden gene controlling face and leg marking size. PATN2: a hidden leopard complex modifier with no real-world testable equivalent, added to account for unexplained blanket patterns. Snowdrop (sno): a breed-specific modifier (Irish Cob) with no known real-world basis.
⚠ lethal combo Frame Overo (OLW) is lethal when homozygous. Breeding two OLW carriers gives a 25% chance of producing an OLW/OLW foal with Overo Lethal White Syndrome (OLWS). Affected foals are born with an underdeveloped intestinal tract and cannot survie, in-game they are flagged and removed at birth. Breeds confirmed to carry OLW: Mustang, Quarter Horse, Thoroughbred. Additionally, all homozygous white spotting (Wx/Wx) combinations are embryonic lethal and result in a failed covering, except W20/W20, which is the only viable homozygous white spotting in-game.
in-game mechanic Grey (G) works differently in-game than in real life. In reality greying is a gradual progressive process. In HR, once a foal becomes an adult its coat changes to a randomly chosen grey phenotype from that breed's pool (dapple grey, steel grey, fleabitten, white, etc.). Grey foals are born hyperpigmented with darker coats than normal and may show grey goggles around the eyes. Grey overrides all other colour expression visually at adulthood. Similarly, Roan (RN) does not show on foals in-game, the roan coat only appears when the horse turns 3 years old.
shared locus Cream (CR) and Pearl (prl) share the same locus. A horse can be CR/prl (one of each), but cannot be CR/CR and prl/prl simultaneously. A single pearl allele (prl/n) has no visible effect on its own, pearl only dilutes when homozygous (prl/prl) or paired with cream (CR/prl), producing a pseudo-double-dilute phenotype similar to a double cream. Breeds with both CR and prl visible: Irish Cob, Lusitano, PRE, Quarter Horse, Mustang.
in-game mechanic Sooty (STY) is dominant on bay-based coats, bay (A), wild bay (A+), seal bay (At), and their cream dilutes (buckskin, seal buckskin): a single copy (STY/n) is sufficient to display sooty shading. On chestnut/red-based coats STY is recessive in most breeds, requiring two copies (STY/STY) to show. Black (a/a) horses never express sooty regardless of copy count, sooty is fully masked on a black base but can still be inherited. Exceptions to the recessive-on-red rule: Lusitano, Trakehner, Oldenburger, and Haflinger treat STY as incomplete dominant on chestnut, meaning STY/n and STY/STY produce different visible expressions (red flaxen remains recessive in Oldenburger and Trakehner. Only Lusitano stays incomplete dominant on red flaxen). Additional masking: sooty is hidden on foals, under Grey (G), on double dilutes (Cr/Cr, Cr/prl, prl/prl, mu/mu), and under Dun in all breeds except Kathiawari (the only breed with sooty dun coats).
